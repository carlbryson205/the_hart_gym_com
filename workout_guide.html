WORKOUT INSPIRATION

Workout Splits

  • Full Body Split — Trains your entire body in one workout and is ideal for beginners building overall strength.
  • Upper / Lower Split — Separates training days into upper-body and lower-body workouts for balanced recovery.
  • Push / Pull / Legs (PPL) — Divides workouts by pushing muscles, pulling muscles, and leg muscles across multiple days.
  • Arms & Shoulders — Focuses on biceps, triceps, and shoulder muscles to improve upper-body definition.
  • Chest & Back — Trains the primary upper-body push and pull muscles for strength and posture.
  • Legs — Targets quads, hamstrings, glutes, and calves for lower-body strength and endurance.
  • Machine-Only Beginner Split — Uses only gym machines for a safe and easy introduction to strength training.

Muscle Groups

  • Chest — Builds pushing strength for movements like pressing and pushing.
  • Back — Improves posture and pulling strength for daily movement and lifting.
  • Shoulders — Supports arm movement and upper-body stability.
  • Arms — Includes biceps and triceps for lifting, pulling, and pushing strength.
  • Legs — Powers walking, running, jumping, and lower-body strength.
  • Core / Abs — Stabilizes the body and supports balance, posture, and power.

Types of Training

  • Strength Training — Builds muscle strength using resistance and weight.
  • Cardio Training — Improves heart health and burns calories through continuous movement.
  • Endurance Training — Increases your ability to exercise longer without fatigue.
  • Fat Loss Training — Focuses on calorie burning and body-fat reduction.
  • Muscle Building (Hypertrophy) — Increases muscle size and definition through resistance training.
  • Functional Training — Improves everyday movement, balance, and coordination.
