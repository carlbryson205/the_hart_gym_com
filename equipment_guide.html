Hart GYM Equipment Guide

Key Tips for Using Gym Machines

  • Adjust Each Machine Properly: Ensure seats, pads, and attachments align with your body size to prevent injury and maximize effectiveness
  • Start with Light Weights: Practice proper form before increasing resistance. Begin with 10–15 reps at a manageable weight
  • Use Smooth, Controlled Movements: Avoid jerky or rushed exercises to focus on the target muscles and reduce risk of injury
  • Focus on Form: Keep your back straight, avoid locking joints, and engage the intended muscles with each rep
  • Leverage Machine Help: Assisted pull-up and dip machines are excellent for building strength gradually if bodyweight exercises are challenging

Lower Body Machines

  • Hip Adductor (Inner Thigh)

    Sit with your back against the pad and legs on the outside of the pads. Squeeze your legs together slowly, then return with control.

  • Hip Abductor (Outer Thigh)

    Sit upright with your legs inside the pads. Push your legs outward against the resistance, then return slowly.

  • Leg Extension (Quads)

    Sit with your shins under the pad and extend your legs upward until they are straight. Lower slowly to complete the rep.

  • Leg Curl (Hamstrings – Seated)

    Sit with your legs under the pad and curl your heels back toward the seat. Control the movement back to the start.

  • Kneeling Leg Curl

    Kneel on the pad with one leg hooked under the roller. Curl your heel upward toward your glutes, then lower slowly.

  • Leg Press

    Sit with your feet shoulder-width apart on the platform. Push the platform away using your legs, then return slowly until knees are bent.

  • Glute Machine

    Place one foot on the platform behind you and keep your chest forward. Push your leg straight back, squeezing your glutes.

  • Prone Leg Curl

    Lie face down with your heels under the pad. Curl your heels toward your glutes, then lower slowly.

  • V Squat

    Stand with your shoulders under the pads and feet on the platform. Squat straight down, then push back up through your heels.

  • Seated Calf Extension

    Place the balls of your feet on the platform with pads on your thighs. Raise your heels upward, then lower slowly.

  • Hip Thrust Machine

    Sit with your upper back against the pad and feet flat on the platform. Drive your hips upward until fully extended, then lower under control.

  • Hack Squat

    Place your back against the pad and feet on the platform. Lower into a squat, then push straight upward.

Arm Machines

  • Bicep Curl Machine

    Sit with your arms on the pads and grip the handles. Curl upward toward your shoulders, then lower slowly.

  • Tricep Extension Machine

    Grip the handles above or in front of you. Push downward until arms are straight, then return slowly.

Chest & Shoulder Machines

  • Chest Press

    Sit with handles at chest height and press forward until your arms are extended. Return slowly.

  • Incline Chest Press

    Sit with the seat angled back and press upward and forward. Slowly bring the handles back to chest level.

  • Shoulder Press

    Sit with handles at shoulder height and press straight upward. Lower slowly back to the start.

  • Pec Fly / Rear Delt

    For chest fly, bring the handles together in front of you; for rear delt, reverse the motion and pull outward. Control both directions.

  • Seated Bench Press

    Sit with handles near chest height and press forward until arms are extended. Bring back under control.

Back Machines

  • Plated Lat Pulldown

    Pull the bar down toward your upper chest while keeping your back straight. Slowly return to full arm extension.

  • High Row

    Pull the handles back toward your chest while squeezing your shoulder blades. Slowly extend your arms forward.

  • Seated Row

    Pull the handles toward your stomach with elbows close to your sides. Slowly extend forward.

  • Lat Pulldown

    Pull the bar down to chest level using your back and arms. Return slowly to the top.

  • Back Extension

    Lock your feet in and hinge forward at the waist. Raise your torso back to a straight position.

Core Machines

  • V Crunch

    Sit back against the pad and grip the handles. Crunch forward using your abs, then return slowly.

  • Abdominal Crunch

    Sit with your feet planted and hands on the handles. Crunch your upper body forward using your core.

Assistance & Strength Equipment

  • Dip/Chin Assist

    Select your weight, place knees on the pad, and perform dips or pull-ups with assisted resistance.

  • Smith Machine

    Position the bar on the tracks and unlock it by rotating. Perform controlled squats, presses, or lunges.

  • Power Rack

    Set the barbell on safety hooks at your desired height. Use for free-weight squats, presses, and deadlifts.

  • Dual Adjustable Pulley

    Adjust the pulleys to any height for cable exercises like rows, flys, curls, and triceps extensions.

  • Lat Pulldown / Seated Row Combo

    Use the top bar for lat pulldowns and the lower cable for seated rows. Control both pulling motions.

Cardio Machines

  • Treadmill

    Step onto the belt and start at a slow speed. Increase speed gradually for walking, jogging, or running.

  • Stair Master

    Step steadily in place as the stairs rotate beneath you. Keep your posture upright and hands light on rails.

  • Elliptical

    Step onto the pedals and move arms and legs in a smooth forward motion. Adjust resistance for intensity.

  • Bike

    Sit with feet on pedals and start cycling. Adjust resistance and seat height for comfort.

  • Rower

    Push with your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle to your chest. Reverse the motion to reset.

  • Ladder (Agility Ladder)

    Step quickly in and out of each square using fast footwork drills. Focus on balance and coordination.

Free Weight Areas

  • Flat Bench

    Lie back with feet flat and perform presses with dumbbells or a barbell. Keep your back stable and controlled.

  • Platform

    Use this area for deadlifts, Olympic lifts, and heavy compound movements. Maintain proper posture at all times.

  • Dumbbells (5–110 lbs)

    Select a comfortable weight and use for presses, curls, rows, lunges, and more. Always lift with control.
